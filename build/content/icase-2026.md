Institutionalizing “Receita Simples”: Implementing a Plain-Language Prescription Tool in the Federal District Health Secretariat (Brazil) 

This presentation describes the development and institutional adoption of Receita Simples, a plain-language prescription tool designed to improve medication understanding, adherence, and patient safety. Originating in Primary Health Care and later incorporated into official policy by the Federal District Health Secretariat, the initiative demonstrates how low-cost communication technologies can support health literacy and accessibility at scale.

Presented in Concurrent Seminar 17 (Communication).

Scientific Production and Organization of Educational Products in Escola de Pacientes DF: An Integrated Teaching–Service–Community Experience in Primary Health Care

This presentation examines a decade-long experience in organizing scientific production and educational products within Primary Health Care. The study highlights how structured workflows, student engagement, and teaching–service–community integration contributed to research dissemination, professional competency development, and educational innovation.

Presented in Concurrent Seminar 52 (Industry–Education Links).

SimulaPacientes: AI-Generated Digital Patients for Health Sciences Education

This presentation introduces SimulaPacientes, a library of AI-generated digital patients developed to support clinical reasoning and communication skills in health sciences education. Using structured prompts and Google Gemini “Gems,” the project offers a scalable and low-cost simulation model that expands access to technology-enhanced learning while highlighting challenges related to assessment, governance, and educational quality.

Presented in Concurrent Seminar 56 (Artificial Intelligence).

Health Education, NCD Policies and Governance: Developing a Trilingual Search Strategy for a Scoping Review

This presentation presents the development of a trilingual search strategy connecting health education, noncommunicable diseases, public policies, and governance. The work seeks to build an integrative framework linking science education, health literacy, equity, and policy implementation, supporting curriculum development, professional training, and educational innovation in Primary Health Care. Presented in Concurrent Seminar 58 (Communication).
